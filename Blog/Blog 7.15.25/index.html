On Getting Overwhelmed.

July 15, 2025

I get overwhelmed pretty easily, I'm even overwhelmed right now while writing this post. Some days it feels like I'll never stop being overwhelmed. There are, in this instance, a number of reasons that neatly explain why I am overwhelmed. I just started a new job, I just moved into an apartment with my girlfriend and it's a bit of a mess, or I just cooked us dinner but we only had vanilla oat milk instead of regular oatmilk so the creamy shrimp pasta tastes weirdly sweet, to name a few. These are the reasons why I'm overwhelmed in this moment but those things will pass and soon there will be 3 more reasons why I'm overwhelmed and sometimes it just feels so inescapable.

There is hope, though, because if there are reasons then those reasons must have solutions or resolutions and if I can just solve and resolve then my overwhelm will pass and everything will be better. Problem solved, thank you for reading, and no, I will not be taking notes on my coping strategies. Okay, so obviously that doesn't really work. It should work, in theory, but in practice I find myself too overwhelmed to be able to actually solve any of the problems. It feels like the only way to stop being overwhelmed is to resolve the stressful situation, in order to solve the situation I need to calm down, in order to calm down I need to do something enjoyable and distracting, the only way to have fun and enjoy myself is to not have responsibilities, and the biggest responsibility I have at that moment is resolving the stressful situation. And then we find ourselves in a loop of my own design, unable to escape.

I believe I may be autistic, I would never say that of course because I don't have a diagnosis. Don't get me wrong, I fully support people who self diagnose with autism, it is completely valid and cool. I'm just not allowed to because the rules of life are different for me and for me it is not cool. I know that that's silly, obviously, and if I think I have autism I should just admit it to myself. I get close, sometimes, I'll make a joke about it with my girlfriend and then feel slightly guilty for pretending I have autism even though I'm just being myself. And sure I scored on the highest possible end of "likely for autism" on the AQ-10, RAADS-R, and CAT-Q tests but those don't count towards convincing my brain because they were taken on the internet instead of administered by a professional. This is the source of a catch-22 that I regularly find myself in, if I admit I have autism I feel guilty for faking having autism, but if I don't admit that I have autism then I feel guilty for perpetuating the idea that a self diagnosis is not a real diagnosis. It makes things very difficult sometimes

When I'm cooking, if I drop something or if I add the wrong ingredient, it can ruin the entire rest of my night. That's kinda what happened tonight, I only had vanilla milk so that's what I used. I tried to pretend it was okay but I served it and I just couldn't get over the smell. I went to my room and ate my food alone while watching terrible YouTube videos that I didn't care about because it was easier than hearing my thoughts. All of that falls on the better end of my reactions to messing up. On the other end, it may be the straw that breaks the camel's back even though there isn't really all that much straw on the camel's back in the first place and maybe the camel is just being a bit dramatic. When I am in a stressful situation and something else stressful happens before I have processed the situation I am already in I am overcome with a sudden uncontrollable urge to shut down entirely. I'm realizing I should probably write a whole other essay just on this because I think I just have too much to say so if you're in the future and interested in reading that hopefully I remember to link it here.

This post is getting a bit long so I think I'm going to just wrap it up now. I'm not overwhelmed anymore, at least not as much as when I started. I think what helped was writing this giant ramble of a blog post without editing it at all. I'm listening to soft instrumental music on Spotify too which is nice. I don't know if I'll ever be able to figure out how to solve my overwhelm problem, at least not without therapy, but I am hopeful that someday it might get better. I'm hoping this new job helps ease it a bit since I will no longer be interacting with 200+ incoming community college students every week. Maybe I'll make a follow up on this post some day if I find a solution but for now I just want to thank you. Thank you for letting me ramble on until I felt better, it means a lot, even if you didn't actually read this whole post.
