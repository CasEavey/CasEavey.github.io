I'm Bad at a Lot of Things.

July 15, 2025

My birthday was a few days ago, July 18th, I had a good time and got to see some family members that I don't typically get to see. I also got a few presents, as one does on their birthday, and I am very appreciative of all of them. My brother and his girlfriend got me Legend of Zelda Echos of Wisdom, my grandma got me a Target gift card, and my mom and step dad got me a toolbox. All of these are great presents, a gift card is practical, I asked for the toolbox specifically, and I have been loving playing Echos of Wisdom these past few days.

However, the present that I was most excited for, the one I asked for specifically and have been wanting for years, is my new banjo. This gift came from my dad and my step mom who I made sure to tell a few weeks early that I wanted it and to remind them about it whenever I saw them. It is a beautiful banjo that they bought used from a local guitar store and I genuinely was blown away when I first saw it. It is more than I could've wanted in every way and I am so proud every time that I get to pull it out of it's case and play it. That is, until I actually start playing it.

What I'm trying to say is, I'm not very good at playing the banjo. In my defense I got the banjo 5 days ago and have never played a string instrument before but that doesn't really change the fact that all I'm doing is strumming and not even that well. My girlfriend will disagree, I know, she's always telling me how good it sounds and that I'm learning very quickly and I believe her but that even still, it's hard to be bad at something.

I've been bad at a lot of things in my life, mostly due to my having ADHD and not sticking with any project long enough to become good at it. I'm used to being a beginner, in some way's it's where I feel most comfortable. Last year I got really into embroidery for a bit, the year before that it was bookbinding. The things I do that I'm most proud of are game development and woodworking. A couple examples of those projects I'm most proud of are this Game Jam game that I made last year and this coffee table that I built for my girlfriend's apartment.

Those are the things I'm most proud of, definitely, but that doesn't mean that I'm good at game development or woodworking. I would say that I'm better than the average person at both but I am in no way better than the average woodworker or game developer. Part of this is, of course, because I am not a woodworker and I am not a game developer, I am a staff accountant for a movie theater that does woodworking and game development in their free time. That's what I need to remind myself, when I'm looking at my table and I notice the finish that pooled up in little stalactites on the bottom edges of the table, I'm a hobbyist, not a professional. I need to remind myself to be proud of what I've done and not constantly judge myself for what I should've done.

I'm not very good at the banjo, but I'm getting better.
