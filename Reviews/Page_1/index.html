Chick-fil-a, Chicken Minis

4/23/2026

To preface this review I would like to take a quick moment to address the controversies of eating at Chick-fil-a and my, very brief, oppinions on the matter. I will not be going into too much detail here, if there is interest for it I may do a seperate review of Chick-fil-a as a company at a later time.

As I'm sure many of you know, Chick-fil-a has been known to donate to organizations and charities that support or are activily involved in anti-LGBTQ movements. As a member of the LGBTQ community in both the T and Q variety I obviously do not support these choices and have boycotted Chick-fil-a for some time. As an Autistic person, I also very often struggle with feeding myself in the mornings, because of this I often find myself eating fast food for breakfast. I really don't like that I have to do this but the most important thing for me is that I sustain myself. Recently I moved to a new appartment and have a new route to work, this route takes me past only 1 fast food restaurant and all others are very out of the way, unfortunately that restaurant is Chick-fil-a. After doing a bit of research I found that Chick-fil-a made changes to their donations policy and no longer donate to anti LGBTQ organizations. I am somebody who believes in forgiveness and, due to my new route to work, I have decided to eat there on days when I am unable to feed myself. If you would like to read a bit more about those policy changes I will link the Wikipedia here.

Alright, now on to my review! I've taken up a lot of space here writing about Chick-fil-a so I'm going to try to be brief with my review. Ultimately, I did not like the Chicken Minis, they were quite the disappointment. The nuggets themselves are fine given that they are quite literally just the chicken nuggets that they serve during lunch but the bread that holds the Minis was downright inedible. The bread was very doughy and somewhat bland, the box comes with 4 Chicken Minis and I ended up eating 2 intentionally and the last 2 I just took out the nugget and threw away the bread. As I said the nuggets are good but they just make me wish I could order regular chicken nuggets for breakfast and skip the dough. Maybe I can, I'll have to ask next time. I definitely won't be getting the Chicken Minis again, 2.5 stars from me.

Ornery Beer Company, Penne Pesto Pasta

4/19/2026

A parmesan pesto cream cream sauce with "grape tomatoes," peas, asparagus, and artichoke hearts.

The pasta sauce was absolutely delicious but there were so many things going on it was too much. If it was any one or 2 of those vegetables it would've been fine but every single bite had a new texture, new shapes, and new flavor. I would even say that the dish was improved by the fact that they forgot to add the grape tomatoes, hence the quotation marks, though I do really love tomatoes, especially in a past dish, so the improvement in texture is paired with a dissapointment in flavor. Overall the dish was overwhelming but enjoyable nonetheless. I added chicken to the pasta which was also good, though a bit dry. I would come back to the restaurant, I probably would not get the penne pesto pasta again, a clean 3.5 stars from me.

The Wild Wild West

4/14/2026

The Wild Wild West is a TV show produced by CBS and ran for four seasons from 1965-1969, the show is a combination of the western, spy, and sci-fi genres. The show was created by Michael Garrison with the general concept being "James Bond but in the Wild West." The show stars Robert Conrad as James West and Ross Martin as Artemus Gordon, two of the best Secret Service agents working under President Ulysses S. Grant. West and Gordon travel across the Post Civil War United States in their own personal train, The Wanderer, protecting The President and The United States from anything that may threaten their safety or the safety of the American People. The series was ultimately cancelled by CBS in 1969 in hopes to appease the government in their war on TV violence, rather than due to low ratings

The violence in The Wild Wild West would be considered tame compared to the media we are exposed to today, there is not even blood or any harsh language to be found. The "violence" of The Wild Wild West is actually one of my favorite parts of the show, every scene feels real and the fights are beautifully choreographed. It is well known that Robert Conrad actually did all of his own stunts for the show and choreographed them as well.

The Wild Wild West was also a fairly progressive show at the time featuring minority actors in some very prominent roles. One episode, "The Night of the Returning Dead," prominently features a black man who the characters are friends with and, at the end of the episode, invite to join them in working for the Secret Service. Another prominent example of positive minority representation is the recurring villain, Dr. Miguelito Loveless, a genius mad scientist born with dwarfism. The dwarfism of the character is never shown to be much of a problem for the doctor and he is treated simply as a villian and more than worthy adversary for James West, nothing less. Of the episodes that I have seen, the only time dwarfism really matters to any of the characters is when one character, an obese women who is working for Loveless, expresses that he is somebody who can understand what it is like to be treated differently by society. Robert Conrad became quick friends with Michael Dunn, the actor who portrays Loveless, and the character was brought back time and again, appearing in a total of 10 episodes.

Unfortunately, The Wild Wild West does have its faults and my review would not be fair without mentioning them. Many of these faults, I believe, can be attributed to the time in which the show was being made, though that does not excuse them. The primary thing that one would notice while watching this show is the repeated use of white actors in brown face portraying indigenous peoples in this show. I can understand why anybody would be turned off of watching this show because of this and I do wish that they had been able to portray indegenous characters/actors with as much respect that they did the black actors in the show. At this point I am only part of the way through season two and so I can not speak on future episodes and if they improve on this but I will try to update the review if that ends up being the case.

I have been watching The Wild Wild West every weekday at 9pm on live TV through on the channel W.E.S.T. (Western Entertainment Series Television). getting to watch the show in this way has been an absolute pleasure, every day I get so excited to watch the next episode and I don't get burned out due to binging the show. Since I started watching I have bought the DVD box set but I still choose to watch it live as the commercials offer nice breaks to do some dishes or work on my computer, I only watch the DVDs when there is a storm interfering with the antenna or when I am out of town or otherwise unable to watch the show live. I am happy to give this show 5/5 stars, despite it's faults this show is funny and progressive for the time it was made, I would definitely rank this as my new favorite TV show and look forward to continuing watching the series in the future.

New Beginnings, Broadmoor

4/13/2026

New Beginnings is a quiet little diner on the corner of Broadmoor and 44th Street, in Kentwood Michigan. New Beginnings is an interesting restaurant for me to review because it would not be my first choice of restaurant if I were to judge it solely based upon the quality of it's food, however, when looking at the restaurant as a whole I would consider it one of the best in West Michigan.

Since I have already brought it up I will begin my review by discussing the food. New Beginnings serves the traditional food that you would expect to see at a diner, that being eggs, meats, pancakes, omelettes, and breakfast foods of the like, as well as a variety of lunch options such as burgers, soups, and sandwiches. The food at New Beginnings is typically of good quality, with some stand out bests and only a couple that should be avoided. My favorites are the chorizo omelette, corned beef hash with scrambled eggs, or their chicken noodle soup. All of their omelettes will satisfy you as well as any of their egg based breakfast options, I'm not the biggest fan of a sweet breakfast but the time that I ordered pancakes I did enjoy it. The only items that I would recommend against getting would be their club sub and their biscuits and gravy, I am a big of biscuits and gravy but these ones feel bland and the gravy too thick, as for the club, I would recommend ordering their three layer club instead of the club sub. Overall I do enjoy the food at New Beginnings with the only caveat being that you may need to add your own salt, this can be forgiven, though, as their clientelle tends to be on the more elderly end of the spectrum and likely would want to control their salt levels.

The thing that really makes New Beginnings stand out would be the staff. I have never had a bad interaction with a single employee here, the waitresses are attentive, funny, and friendly and the bussers are hard working and friendly as well. The primary reason that I continue to return to eat at New Beginnings week after week is getting to see the waitresses, getting to build a friendship with the waitresses here has been great, getting welcomed by name as I find my seat, getting asked about my life and talking about theirs, I know that I am welcomed here and it makes me want to come back again and again. If you are looking for somewhere to become a regular I can recommend no place better.

My final review breakdown is this: I give the food 3.5/5 stars, I give the atmosphere 4/5 stars, I give the cleanliness of the restaurant 5/5 stars, and I give the waitresses a solid 10/5 stars. That all brings the total, rounded down, to a clean 5/5 stars, I would highly recommend giving this restaurant a shot if you're ever driving by in the morning!
